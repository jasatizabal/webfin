Chocolate Mousse

Fifth Recipe

 Posted on February 10, 2020  |  1 minutes  |  103 words  |  

Ingredients:

  • 200 g homemade chocolate
  • 300 ml heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: Melt the homemade chocolate using a double boiler method.
  2. Whip the cream: In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream (and sugar, if using) until stiff peaks form.
  3. Combine: Once the chocolate is melted and slightly cooled, gently fold it into the whipped cream in small batches until well combined.
  4. Chill: Spoon the mousse into serving glasses or bowls and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until set.
  5. Serve: Top with grated chocolate, whipped cream, or fresh berries before serving.

Chocolate Lava Cake

Fourth Recipe

 Posted on February 4, 2020  |  1 minutes  |  151 words  |  

Ingredients:

  • 100 g homemade chocolate
  • 100 g butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 50 g sugar
  • 50 g flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Grease and flour four small ramekins or baking cups.
  2. Melt the chocolate: Melt the homemade chocolate and butter together using a double boiler.
  3. Mix the eggs and sugar: In a bowl, whisk the eggs, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and frothy.
  4. Combine: Add the melted chocolate-butter mixture to the egg-sugar mixture, then fold in the flour and a pinch of salt.
  5. Bake: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared ramekins. Bake for about 12-14 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center is still soft.
  6. Serve: Let the cakes cool for 1-2 minutes, then carefully invert them onto plates. The centers should be molten and gooey—serve with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream!

Chocolate-Dipped Fruit

Third Recipe

 Posted on January 25, 2020  |  1 minutes  |  115 words  |  

Ingredients:

  • 150 g homemade chocolate
  • Fresh fruit (strawberries, bananas, orange slices, apple slices, etc.)
  • Chopped nuts or sprinkles (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: Melt the homemade chocolate as before, using the double boiler method.
  2. Prepare the fruit: Wash and dry your chosen fruit thoroughly.
  3. Dip the fruit: Dip each piece of fruit into the melted chocolate, coating about halfway or completely, depending on your preference.
  4. Add toppings (optional): If you like, you can roll the dipped fruit in chopped nuts or sprinkles for added texture and flavor.
  5. Chill: Place the dipped fruit on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for about 30 minutes or until the chocolate hardens.
  6. Serve: Enjoy as a healthy yet indulgent treat!

Chocolate Bark

Second Recipe

 Posted on January 21, 2020  |  1 minutes  |  122 words  |  

Ingredients:

  • 200 g homemade chocolate
  • 1/2 cup mixed nuts (almonds, pistachios, hazelnuts, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup dried fruit (raisins, cranberries, apricots, etc.)
  • Sea salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: Melt the homemade chocolate using the double boiler method.
  2. Prepare the pan: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  3. Pour and spread: Pour the melted chocolate onto the prepared baking sheet and spread it out into a thin, even layer.
  4. Add toppings: Sprinkle the mixed nuts, dried fruit, and sea salt (if using) evenly over the melted chocolate.
  5. Chill: Refrigerate for about 1-2 hours until the chocolate hardens.
  6. Break into pieces: Once firm, break the bark into irregular pieces.
  7. Serve: Enjoy your homemade chocolate bark as a snack or gift!

Chocolate Truffles

First Recipe

 Posted on January 18, 2020  |  1 minutes  |  160 words  |  

Ingredients:

-200 g homemade chocolate (from the recipe above) -100 ml heavy cream -1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional) -Cocoa powder, chopped nuts, or coconut flakes for coating

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate: Gently melt the homemade chocolate using a double boiler method (as explained in the original recipe).
  2. Heat the cream: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Do not let it boil.
  3. Combine: Pour the hot cream over the melted chocolate and stir until smooth and combined. Add vanilla extract if using.
  4. Chill: Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for about 2 hours or until firm enough to scoop.
  5. Shape truffles: Once chilled, use a spoon or melon baller to scoop out small portions and roll them into balls with your hands.
  6. Coat: Roll the truffles in cocoa powder, chopped nuts, or shredded coconut.
  7. Serve: Store in the fridge until ready to serve. Enjoy these decadent bites!
