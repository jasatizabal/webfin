First Post! How to make homemade chocolate.

 Posted on January 5, 2020  |  2 minutes  |  270 words  |  

First here is the list of ingredients:

  • 100 g cocoa butter (this is the base ingredient for making chocolate)
  • 50 g unsweetened cocoa powder (make sure it’s pure cocoa)
  • 2-3 tablespoons of sugar (adjust to taste, or use honey or agave syrup)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt (optional, enhances the flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the cocoa butter:

Place the cocoa butter in a heatproof bowl (glass or metal works well). Melt it using a double boiler: fill a saucepan with water and place the bowl with the cocoa butter on top, making sure the bowl doesn’t touch the water. Heat over low heat, stirring until the cocoa butter is fully melted. Add the cocoa powder:

  1. Add the cocoa powder:

Once the cocoa butter has melted, add the unsweetened cocoa powder and mix well with a whisk until fully combined. Make sure there are no lumps. Sweeten the chocolate:

  1. Sweeten the chocolate:

Add the sugar (or your preferred sweetener) and mix well until it dissolves completely. If you like, you can also add vanilla extract or a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor. Pour into molds:

  1. Pour it into molds:

Once everything is well combined, pour the chocolate mixture into molds. If you don’t have specific chocolate molds, you can use a small baking tray lined with parchment paper. Let it cool at room temperature and then refrigerate for about 2 hours, or until the chocolate hardens completely. Unmold and enjoy:

  1. Unmold and enjoy!:

Once the chocolate has solidified, carefully remove it from the molds. And that’s it! You now have delicious homemade chocolate, ready to enjoy.
